Sigiriya

Sigiriya is a small town located in the Central Province of Sri Lanka, and is home to one of the country's most iconic and popular attractions: the Sigiriya Rock Fortress. This ancient fortress was built in the 5th century AD by King Kasyapa, who used it as his palace and fortress. Today, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is considered to be one of the most impressive historical and archaeological sites in Sri Lanka.
The Sigiriya Rock Fortress is a massive rock formation that rises over 200 meters above the surrounding jungle. It is surrounded by moats, walls, and gardens, and is home to a number of impressive features, including the famous frescoes of the Sigiriya Maidens, the Mirror Wall, and the Lion's Paw entrance. Climbing to the top of the fortress is a challenging but rewarding experience, with incredible views of the surrounding landscape.

Sri Dalada Maligawa

The Temple of the Tooth Relic, also known as Sri Dalada Maligawa, is a Buddhist temple located in the city of Kandy in Sri Lanka. It is considered to be one of the most important religious sites in the country and is believed to house a tooth relic of the Buddha.
The temple complex is located next to Kandy Lake and consists of several buildings, including the main temple, the shrine room where the tooth relic is kept, and several other smaller temples and museums. The temple is surrounded by a wall and is accessed through a large gate with intricate carvings and decorations.
Visitors to the Temple of the Tooth Relic can take part in a variety of activities, including observing the daily rituals and offerings, exploring the various buildings and exhibits, and learning about the history and significance of the tooth relic. There are also regular cultural performances and festivals held at the temple throughout the year, which provide a unique insight into Sri Lankan traditions and culture.
When visiting the Temple of the Tooth Relic, it's important to dress modestly and remove your shoes before entering the shrine room where the tooth relic is kept. It's also recommended to visit the temple early in the day to avoid crowds, and to hire a local guide to provide a deeper understanding of the temple's history and significance.

Anuradhapura

Anuradhapura is one of the most important ancient cities in Sri Lanka and is located in the north-central part of the island. It was the first capital of Sri Lanka and served as the political and religious center of the country for over 1,300 years, until it was abandoned in the 10th century AD. Today, Anuradhapura is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and attracts visitors from around the world who come to see its ancient ruins, temples, and other historical and cultural landmarks. Some of the popular sites to visit in Anuradhapura include, Ruwanweli Sey, Sri Maha Bodhi, Jetavanaramaya, Thuparamaya, Mirisawetiya etc.

Arugam Bay

Arugam Bay is a small coastal town located on the eastern coast of Sri Lanka, and is a popular destination for surfing and beach activities. The town is surrounded by lush greenery and is known for its laid-back atmosphere and beautiful beaches.
One of the main attractions in Arugam Bay is the surf, which draws surfers from around the world. The town has a number of surf schools and rental shops, making it a great place for both experienced surfers and beginners. The waves in Arugam Bay are best from May to September, with some surf breaks working well into October.

Galle Fort

Galle Fort is a historic fortified city located on the southwestern coast of Sri Lanka. It was originally built by the Portuguese in the 16th century and later expanded by the Dutch in the 17th century. Today, the fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is considered to be one of the best-preserved examples of colonial architecture in Asia.
One of the highlights of visiting Galle Fort is the opportunity to explore the historic buildings and landmarks. Some of the most popular attractions include the Dutch Reformed Church, the Galle National Museum, and the lighthouse, which offers stunning views of the surrounding ocean and coastline.
Another popular activity in Galle Fort is shopping and dining. The town has a variety of boutique shops and cafes, selling everything from local crafts and souvenirs to international fashion brands and gourmet food. The town also has a lively nightlife scene, with several bars and restaurants offering live music and entertainment.

Jaffna

Jaffna is a city located in the northernmost part of Sri Lanka. It is the capital city of the Northern Province and is home to a predominantly Tamil population. Jaffna is also famous for its unique cuisine, with dishes such as crab curry and traditional Tamil dishes like thosai, idli and vadai. The Nallur Kandaswamy Temple, a famous Hindu temple, is located in Jaffna and is one of the most important religious sites in Sri Lanka.

Yala National Park

Yala National Park is a popular wildlife sanctuary located in the southeastern region of Sri Lanka. It is the second-largest national park in the country and covers an area of 979 square kilometers. The park is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including elephants, leopards, sloth bears, crocodiles, and a variety of bird species. Yala is particularly renowned for its leopard population, with the park having one of the highest densities of leopards in the world. The park has several zones that visitors can explore, including Block 1, which is the most popular area for safari tours. Visitors can also opt to camp overnight within the park or stay in nearby accommodations. While visiting Yala National Park, it's important to follow the park rules and regulations to ensure the safety of both visitors and wildlife.
